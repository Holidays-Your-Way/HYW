Loews Sapphire Falls Resort

A relaxed, tropical resort with a lovely laid back atmosphere.

Loews Sapphire Falls Resort

Sapphire Falls has this gorgeous “tropical escape” feel to it. The moment you arrive, everything feels calm, bright and welcoming. Palm trees, soft colours, water views and that lovely, easygoing atmosphere that makes you instantly slow down a little. It’s one of those hotels that feels like a proper resort, not just somewhere to sleep between park days.

What makes Sapphire Falls stand out is how beautifully balanced it is. It’s more polished than the Value and Prime Value hotels, but without the luxury price tag of the Premier resorts. It works brilliantly for families, couples and adults who want something a bit more refined, but still relaxed and friendly.

And here’s something a lot of people don’t realise: Sapphire Falls has family suites. They’re not heavily advertised, but they’re a fantastic option if you want more space. The kids’ suites have a separate bedroom for children, which makes mornings, bedtimes and longer stays so much easier. They’re ideal for families who want a bit more breathing room but still want that relaxed, tropical atmosphere.

The pool area is a real highlight. It’s big, bright and beautifully designed, with a waterslide, plenty of space to lounge, and that lovely tropical landscaping that makes it feel like a proper resort. It’s lively enough to be fun, but never feels chaotic.

The rooms are modern, fresh and comfortable, nothing over the top, just really well done. Lots of natural light, clean design, and a calm colour palette that fits the whole tropical theme. They’re a lovely place to come back to after a day in the parks.

Dining choices are excellent here. Amatista Cookhouse is one of the best table service restaurants at any Universal hotel. It is relaxed, has great food, and it's ideal for a slower evening. There’s also a rum bar, a quick service marketplace, and of course a Starbucks for early mornings.

One of Sapphire Falls’ biggest perks is the water taxi. It’s such a lovely way to get to CityWalk and the parks, calm, scenic and surprisingly quick. You can still use the shuttle buses if you prefer, but most guests end up choosing the boat because it’s just a nicer experience.

Location wise, you’re in a brilliant spot. You can walk to Aventura and Cabana Bay if you fancy a change of scenery, and Volcano Bay is close by too. It’s a great base if you want convenience without being right in the middle of the busiest areas.

Sapphire Falls is perfect if you want a resort that feels relaxed, tropical and a little more polished. It’s great for families who want a step up, couples who want something calmer, and adults who like a resort with atmosphere but not noise, especially with the bonus of family suites that give you extra space without the Premier price tag.

Pros & Considerations

Pros

  • Gorgeous tropical theming and atmosphere
  • Large, beautifully designed pool with a waterslide
  • Family suites available
  • Water taxi to CityWalk and the parks
  • Excellent dining, especially Amatista Cookhouse
  • Great balance of quality and value
  • Calm, relaxed vibe that suits adults and families
  • Modern, comfortable rooms
  • Early park entry included

Considerations

  • Not as luxurious as the Premier hotels
  • No Express Pass included
  • Can feel busy around the pool during peak times
  • Some rooms have longer walks to the lobby or water taxi
  • Not walking distance to the main parks
