Dining at Disney

Everything you need to know about eating at Walt Disney World.

Food Is a Big Part of the Disney Magic

From character breakfasts to themed restaurants and quick bites between rides, dining at Disney is a huge part of the experience. But it can also feel confusing — especially when it comes to booking reservations.

This guide breaks everything down simply, so you know exactly how dining works and how to make the most of it.

Table Service Dining

Table Service restaurants are sit‑down meals where you’re served by a waiter. These include everything from character dining to themed restaurants and signature fine dining.

Booking Reservations

Disney restaurants book up quickly — especially character meals and popular spots like Ohana, California Grill and Cinderella’s Royal Table. Here’s what you need to know:

  • You can book dining 60 days before your trip.
  • If you’re staying in a Disney Resort, you can book 60 days + the next 10 days of your stay.
  • This gives on‑site guests a big advantage for hard‑to‑get reservations.

If you have certain restaurants in mind, let me know — I can help you plan which ones to book and when.

Character Dining

Character meals are incredibly popular with families. They’re a great way to meet characters without queuing in the parks. Options include:

  • Chef Mickey’s
  • Crystal Palace
  • Topolino’s Terrace Breakfast
  • Akershus Royal Banquet Hall
Signature Dining

These are Disney’s premium restaurants — perfect for adults, celebrations or a special night out. Expect amazing food, beautiful views and a more refined atmosphere.

Quick Service Dining

Quick Service restaurants are counter‑service meals — no reservations needed. They’re ideal for families who want flexibility and don’t want to plan every meal in advance.

Mobile Ordering

Most Quick Service locations offer mobile ordering through the My Disney Experience app. This lets you:

  • Order food in advance
  • Choose a pickup time
  • Avoid queues

It’s especially useful during busy times or when you’re trying to squeeze in meals between rides.

Popular Quick Service Spots
  • Flame Tree Barbecue – Animal Kingdom
  • Regal Eagle Smokehouse – EPCOT
  • Woody’s Lunch Box – Hollywood Studios
  • Columbia Harbour House – Magic Kingdom

Snacks & Treats

Disney snacks are iconic — and some are must‑tries! From classic Mickey bars to seasonal treats, there’s always something new to discover.

  • Dole Whip, my personal favourite snack.
  • Mickey Pretzels
  • Churros
  • School Bread (EPCOT Norway Pavilion)
  • Seasonal cupcakes and festival treats

My Top Tips for Disney Dining

  • Book early — especially for character dining and popular restaurants.
  • Be flexible with times; breakfast or late lunches are often easier to book.
  • Don’t overbook — one Table Service meal per day is usually enough.
  • Use mobile ordering to avoid queues at Quick Service locations.
  • Tell me your must‑dos and I’ll help you plan the best way to fit them in.
