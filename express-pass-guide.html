Universal Express Pass Guide

A simple, friendly guide to understanding Express Pass — and when it actually makes a difference.

Universal’s Express Pass is one of the most common things people ask me about. Is it worth it? Do you need it? Does it work at Epic Universe? And what’s the difference between the two types?

This guide breaks everything down in a clear, honest way so you can decide what’s right for your family. Without the confusion.

What Is Universal Express Pass?

Express Pass lets you use a separate, much shorter queue for most rides at Universal Orlando. It doesn’t skip the queue completely, but it usually reduces your wait dramatically, especially on busy days.

The Two Types of Express Pass

1. Universal Express Pass

You can use the Express queue once per ride.

2. Universal Express Pass Unlimited

You can use the Express queue as many times as you like on the same ride. This is the version included with Premier hotels.

Does Epic Universe Use Express Pass?

Yes, but with some important details. Epic Universe does accept Express Pass, but not every ride uses it yet, and the most popular attractions may have limited availability.

The two rides that regularly hit 4+ hour waits are:

  • Harry Potter and the Battle at the Ministry
  • Donkey Kong: Mine‑Cart Madness

These are the ones where Express Pass makes the biggest difference when it’s available.

Which Rides Accept Express Pass?

Here’s a simple breakdown for the three theme parks that use Express Pass.

Universal Studios Florida

  • Despicable Me Minion Mayhem
  • Transformers
  • Revenge of the Mummy
  • Men in Black
  • The Bourne Stuntacular
  • Fast & Furious: Supercharged (still open for now — will close once the new Fast & Furious coaster opens)

Islands of Adventure

  • The Amazing Adventures of Spider‑Man
  • The Incredible Hulk Coaster
  • Skull Island: Reign of Kong
  • Jurassic Park River Adventure
  • Harry Potter and the Forbidden Journey
  • Flight of the Hippogriff
  • Hagrid’s Magical Creatures Motorbike Adventure (varies, it's not always included)

Epic Universe

Express Pass is rolling out gradually. Availability varies by attraction and demand.

How to Get Express Pass for Free

If you stay at a Premier Universal hotel, you get Unlimited Express Pass included for:

  • the day you check in
  • every day of your stay
  • the day you check out

The Premier hotels are:

  • Royal Pacific Resort
  • Hard Rock Hotel
  • Portofino Bay Hotel

Times When Express Pass Makes the Biggest Difference

Express Pass is most useful when:

  • the parks are busy
  • school holidays are in full swing
  • weekends are crowded
  • new attractions have just opened
  • the weather is hot and queues feel longer
  • you want to do a lot in one day
  • you’re visiting Epic Universe and want to avoid the longest waits

The biggest queues in the whole resort are currently:

  • Battle at the Ministry (4+ hours)
  • Donkey Kong: Mine‑Cart Madness (4+ hours)

Times When Express Pass Isn’t Usually Needed

  • outside school holidays
  • if you’re happy to take your time
  • if you’re staying for several days
  • if you’re using Early Park Admission
  • if you’re focusing on shows, dining and atmosphere
  • evenings at Epic Universe (queues often drop later on)

Express Pass vs Early Park Admission

These two work beautifully together. Early Park Admission gets you into selected parks an hour early, and Express Pass reduces your wait times later in the day.

Express Pass for Families

  • No need to buy Express Pass for children under 3.
  • If one person has Express Pass, everyone else needs it too.
  • Express queues are usually much shorter and easier for little ones.
  • Height requirements still apply. An express Pass doesn’t change this.

My Honest Advice

Express Pass is brilliant, but only when you actually need it. If you’re visiting during busy times, want to avoid long queues, or have certain rides you absolutely don’t want to miss, it can make a huge difference.

If you’re visiting at quieter times or you’re happy to take things slowly, you may not need it at all. And if you’re staying in a Premier hotel, enjoy it. It’s one of the best perks Universal offers.
