Surfside Inn & Suites

A bright, beachy, great‑value hotel that just feels easy.

Universal’s Endless Summer Resort – Surfside Inn & Suites

Surfside Inn & Suites is one of those hotels that instantly puts you at ease. It’s bright, cheerful and has that relaxed “we’re on holiday” feeling the moment you walk in. Nothing about it is fussy or over‑the‑top. It’s simple in the nicest way, and that’s exactly why so many people love it.

The whole place has a light, beach vibe. Think soft colours, sunny décor and a really friendly atmosphere. It’s not trying to be a luxury resort, and honestly, that’s part of its charm. It’s clean, it’s comfortable, and it’s brilliant value for money.

One of the biggest reasons people choose Surfside is the two‑bedroom suites. These are such a lifesaver if you’ve got older kids, teens, or just want a bit of breathing space. You get two separate sleeping areas, a little kitchenette with a microwave and fridge, and enough room that you’re not all tripping over each other. For the price, it’s hard to beat.

The pool area is lovely, it's nothing huge or dramatic, but bright, clean and perfect for a cooling down after the parks. It has that relaxed, friendly feel where the kids make friends instantly and parents can actually sit down for five minutes. There’s a pool bar too, which is always a nice bonus.

Food wise, Surfside keeps things simple. The food court has a good mix of quick meals and grab and go bits, which works perfectly when you’re heading out early or coming back tired. And yes, there’s a Starbucks in the lobby, which is always handy for those early park mornings.

Transport is straightforward. You’ve got regular shuttle buses to the parks, and they’re quick and reliable. You don’t have walking paths or water taxis here, but honestly, the buses do the job perfectly and most find it really easy.

Also a lot of people don’t always realise: International Drive is just a short walk away. Meaning even more food options, shops and places to nosey round in the evening. If you like having a bit of choice beyond the hotel food court, Surfside is brilliant for that. You’re close enough to I‑Drive to enjoy it, but far enough away that the hotel still feels calm and relaxed.

If you’re looking for a hotel that’s clean, friendly, great value and genuinely easy to stay in, Surfside is a brilliant choice. It’s not fancy, but it doesn’t need to be. It’s warm, welcoming and gives you everything you need for a comfortable Universal holiday.

Pros & Considerations

Pros

  • One of the best value hotels at Universal
  • Two‑bedroom suites are fantastic for families
  • Bright, cheerful, relaxed atmosphere
  • Starbucks in the lobby
  • Quick, reliable shuttle buses
  • Early park entry included
  • Walking distance to International Drive for extra dining options
  • Great option for larger families on a budget

Considerations

  • Not walking distance to the parks
  • Theming is simple rather than immersive
  • Pool area is smaller than the bigger resorts
  • Fewer dining options on‑site compared to Cabana Bay or Sapphire Falls
  • No water taxi or walking path to the parks
