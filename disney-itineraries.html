Disney Itineraries

Plan the perfect 7, 10 or 14‑day Disney holiday.

Make the Most of Your Disney Holiday

UK guests typically visit Walt Disney World for 7–14 days, which means you can enjoy the parks at a relaxed pace without rushing. These itineraries give you a simple structure for your days, with plenty of time for rest, pool days and exploring Orlando.

Example of 7‑Day Disney Itinerary

  • Day 1 – Magic Kingdom
  • Day 2 – EPCOT
  • Day 3 – Hollywood Studios
  • Day 4 – Water Park (Typhoon Lagoon or Blizzard Beach – Disney usually has one open at a time, check with me for your dates)
  • Day 5 – Animal Kingdom
  • Day 6 – Favourite Park
  • Day 7 – Disney Springs / Resort Day

Example of 10‑Day Disney Itinerary

  • Day 1 – Magic Kingdom
  • Day 2 – Magic Kingdom
  • Day 3 – EPCOT
  • Day 4 – EPCOT
  • Day 5 – Rest Day
  • Day 6 – Hollywood Studios
  • Day 7 – Animal Kingdom
  • Day 8 – Water Park (whichever is open during your stay)
  • Day 9 – Disney Springs
  • Day 10 – Favourite Park

Example of 14‑Day Disney Itinerary

  • Days 1–2 – Magic Kingdom
  • Days 3–4 – EPCOT
  • Day 5 – Rest Day
  • Days 6–7 – Hollywood Studios
  • Days 8–9 – Animal Kingdom
  • Day 10 – Water Park
  • Day 11 – Resort Day
  • Day 12 – Disney Springs
  • Days 13–14 – Favourite Parks

How to Make the Most of Your Days at Disney

Here’s how I personally recommend approaching each park to make your days smoother, avoid unnecessary queues and save money on Lightning Lanes where possible.

Magic Kingdom
  • Head straight to Seven Dwarfs Mine Train at rope drop.
  • If you can get a Lightning Lane for Seven Dwarfs, brilliant — it’s the best use of one here.
  • Peter Pan has long queues so might be worth using a Lightning Lane for it.
  • TRON is another good Lightning Lane option if you fancy it during your visit.
EPCOT
  • Start your day with Guardians of the Galaxy: Cosmic Rewind — it’s the most popular ride in the park, and in my opinion, the BEST ride Disney have ever created.
  • If you can grab a Lightning Lane for Guardians, great — but rope drop works well too.
  • Frozen Ever After and Remy’s Ratatouille Adventure are good second choices.
Hollywood Studios
  • Your big decision is between Slinky Dog Dash and Rise of the Resistance.
  • Rope drop one of them, and use a Lightning Lane for the other if you can.
  • Toy Story Land is best done early before queues build.
Animal Kingdom
  • Go straight to Pandora at rope drop.
  • Ride Flight of Passage first — this saves you buying a Lightning Lane.
  • Then do Na’vi River Journey while you’re already in the land.
Water Parks
  • Remember our UK tickets include water parks, so go amd make the most of them. My favourite is the OG Typhoon Lagoon
  • Disney usually has one water park open at a time for maintenance.
  • Check with me and I’ll confirm whether Typhoon Lagoon or Blizzard Beach is open during your trip.
