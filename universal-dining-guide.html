Universal Orlando Dining Guide

Honest, simple, reliable food advice – from someone who’s actually eaten almost everywhere.

Universal Orlando has loads of dining options across the parks, CityWalk and the hotels. Some are brilliant, some are perfectly fine, and some you’ll forget the second you walk out. It’s theme park food at the end of the day, and everyone’s taste is different. Disney isn’t automatically better either; it’s all personal preference.

But if we’re being honest? Epic Universe is the standout. The theming, the atmosphere, the actual food quality… it’s the only park where I’d genuinely plan a proper sit down meal.

Not everyone hires a car these days, so this guide works whether you’re staying on‑site, relying on transport. I’ll tell you what’s good, what’s reliable, what’s overrated, and where I actually eat.

Universal Studios Florida – Decent, Reliable, but nothing too Fancy

Studios has plenty of options, but it’s not the strongest park for food. Think quick, easy, familiar.

Where I’d Actually Eat

Today Café
My go to here for fresh sandwiches, pastries and coffee. It's quick, reliable and not too heavy.

Leaky Cauldron
Lovely theming, decent food. Great for breakfast or a slower lunch.

Louie’s Italian Restaurant
Simple pizza and pasta. Perfect for families and picky eaters.

Finnegan’s Bar & Grill
A proper sit down pub. Great for adults who want a break and a proper meal.

Richter’s Burger Co.
It’s fine. Good if you just want something quick and familiar.

Snacks Worth Grabbing

  • Giant pretzels
  • any refillable popcorn, but remember, most of it is salted butter movie style popcorn.
  • Minion popcorn buckets, the buckets are soooo cute. But the popcorn is an aquired taste. It is banana flavoured.
  • Ice cream from the San Francisco area

My honest take: Eat early, eat light, or save your appetite for CityWalk.

Islands of Adventure – Good Quick Service, Easy Wins

IOA is brilliant for quick, tasty meals that don’t slow you down.

Where I’d Actually Eat

Thunder Falls Terrace
One of the better quick service restaurants in any theme park. Great chicken, ribs and salads.

Three Broomsticks
Lovely theming, good breakfast, solid lunch.

Croissant Moon Bakery
Perfect for pastries and sandwiches.

The Burger Digs
Reliable, simple, good for picky eaters.

Mythos
Beautiful setting, decent food. Lovely for grown ups, but not essential. Due to close for refurbishment early 2026 date TBC.

Snacks Worth Grabbing

  • Churros
  • Butterbeer (all forms), I prefer the frozen one.
  • Jurassic Park snacks
  • Ice cream from the carts

My honest take: IOA is the easiest park to eat in. It is quick, reliable, no fuss.

Epic Universe – The Best Park for Food (Hands Down)

This is where Universal has really spent money, and it shows. The theming, the atmosphere, the food quality… everything feels a step above.

Celestial Park

The Blue Dragon
Stylish, atmospheric, good food. Lovely for a slower meal.

Atlantis
Seafood with some of the best views in the entire resort, especially at night. Perfect if it’s just us grown ups this time.

Oak & Star Tavern
BBQ style dishes, great portions and a relaxed feel. Brilliant for families, groups and anyone who just wants good, tasty food without any fuss.

Isle of Berk

Mead Hall
Hearty, fun, brilliant theming. Great for families.

Spit Fyre Grill
Quick, easy, tasty.

Hooligan’s Grog & Gruel
Snacks and grab and go. You have to try the mac & cheese cones.

Super Nintendo World

Cute snacks, very themed, very chaotic. Fun for photos, but not somewhere I’d sit down for a full meal.

Dark Universe

Moody snacks and drinks. Great atmosphere, especially after dark.

Snacks Worth Grabbing

  • Epic Universe exclusive treats
  • Themed pastries
  • Night time desserts in Celestial Park

My honest take: Epic Universe is the only park where I’d plan a proper sit down meal.

Volcano Bay – Surprisingly Good (and I Like the Pizza)

Volcano Bay has some great food for a water park.

Kohola Reef Restaurant & Social Club
Great bowls, salads and island style dishes.

Bambu
Quick snacks and lighter bites.

Volcano Bay Pizza
I always pick the pizza here. It's simple, tasty and perfect after a swim.

Waturi Fusion Ice Cream
A must try.

My honest take: Eat when you’re hungry, enjoy the snacks, and save your proper meals for CityWalk or off‑site.

CityWalk – The Best Place for Dinner

CityWalk is where most people end up for dinner, especially if you’re staying on‑site.

Sit Down Restaurants

The Cowfish
Fun, lively, great for families. A very unique take on burgers and sushi.

Bigfire
Lovely for adults, really great atmosphere.

Vivo Italian Kitchen
Fresh pasta, great service.

Antojitos Authentic Mexican Food
Brilliant Mexican food with live music.

NBC Sports Grill & Brew
Huge menu, great atmosphere, brilliant for groups.

Hard Rock Café
Classic, loud, fun, great for families and teens.

Bubba Gump Shrimp Co.
Seafood, big portions, themed fun. I love a crab boil and for a chain restaurant, they do okay.

Quick & Familiar Options

Panda Express
Great for a quick, reliable meal. This is often our go to at the end of the night. As simple cheap and fills a hole.

Burger King Whopper Bar
Perfect for picky eaters and teens.

Red Oven Pizza Bakery
Fast, fresh pizza. Really strong choice for pizza.

Bread Box
Brilliant sandwiches.

Snacks & Desserts

Voodoo Doughnut
A must visit, it isfun, colourful, and great for photos. They even have a donut called Texas, it is huge, hence the name.

Toothsome Chocolate Emporium
Amazing shakes and desserts. They are HUGE, we choose to share as they are so rich and sickly sweet.

Cinnabon
Perfect for a sweet treat. Now this isn't somewhere I would choose, as I hate cinnamon. But it is a huge favourite with my clients.

Parking at CityWalk

Parking prices are correct at time of writing and can change, but as a guide:

  • Standard Parking: £32
  • Prime Parking: £50-60
  • Valet Parking: £32 under 2 hours, $65-75 over 2 hours
  • Oversized Vehicles: £42
  • Free after 6pm – except on any evening events night

Hotel Dining – Quick Overview

Full details are on each resort page, but here’s a quick snapshot.

Best Breakfasts

  • Amatista Cookhouse (Sapphire Falls)
  • Trattoria del Porto (Portofino Bay)
  • Bayliner Diner (Cabana Bay)

Best Grown Ups Meals

  • Strong Water Tavern (Sapphire Falls)
  • Mama Della’s Ristorante (Portofino Bay)
  • The Palm (Hard Rock Hotel)

Best Grab and Go

  • Urban Pantry (Aventura)
  • Pier 8 Market (Dockside)
  • Beach Break Café (Surfside)

My honest take: Hotel dining is great for convenience. But if you have a car, you’ve got endless options off‑site, and is my personal choice.

Dietary Needs

Universal is excellent with allergies and dietary requirements. Always speak to your server, the chef usually comes out and has a chat with you about your requirements. They’re brilliant and will happily walk you through options. Gluten‑free and vegan choices are improving all the time, and mobile ordering can help you check ingredients before you commit.

My Honest Advice

  • Don’t queue 40 minutes for food – mobile order when you can.
  • Epic Universe has the best food by far.
  • Islands of Adventure is great for quick, reliable meals.
  • Universal Studios is fine, but I often eat light here.
  • CityWalk is brilliant for dinner.
  • If you have a car, don’t be afraid to go off‑site.
  • Atlantis at night is stunning if you want a grown ups meal with atmosphere.
  • Voodoo Doughnut is absolutely worth the stop.
