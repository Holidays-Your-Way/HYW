Disney vs Universal – Which First?

A simple guide to choosing the best order for your holiday.

The Big Question for UK Families

When you’re planning a Florida holiday, one of the biggest decisions is whether to visit Walt Disney World or Universal Orlando first. Both are incredible, both offer completely different experiences, and both deserve time to explore properly.

The good news? There’s no wrong answer — but the *right* order can make your holiday feel smoother, more magical and far less tiring.

Start With Disney If…

  • Your family loves characters, parades and classic Disney magic
  • You want a gentle start to your holiday
  • You’re travelling with younger children
  • You want to build up to the big thrill rides
  • You’re staying at a Disney hotel first

Disney is perfect for easing into the holiday — especially after a long‑haul flight. The theming, atmosphere and slower pace help you settle in before tackling the bigger thrills at Universal.

Start With Universal If…

  • Your family loves big coasters and high‑energy attractions
  • You have teens or thrill‑seekers
  • You’re staying at a Universal hotel first
  • You want to begin with shorter park days
  • You’re planning to use Express Pass

Universal is compact, easy to navigate and full of high‑energy fun. Starting here works brilliantly for families who want excitement from day one.

Doing a Split Stay?

Many UK families choose to stay at both Disney and Universal — and this is where the order really matters.

🏰 Start at Disney → Move to Universal

Great for families with younger children or those who want to build up to the big thrills.

🎢 Start at Universal → Move to Disney

Perfect for families who want a high‑energy start and a magical, relaxing finish.

What Affects the Best Order?

  • Your children’s ages – toddlers vs teens makes a big difference
  • Your hotel choices – monorail, Skyliner or Express Pass perks
  • Your park priorities – characters, coasters or Harry Potter
  • Your energy levels – especially after long‑haul travel
  • Your length of stay – 7, 14 or 21 nights

There’s no one‑size‑fits‑all answer — but there *is* a perfect order for your family.

My General Recommendation for UK Families

Most UK families benefit from starting with Disney and ending with Universal — especially if you’re staying at a Premier Universal hotel with Express Pass included. It creates a brilliant “grand finale” to your holiday.
