Cabana Bay Beach Resort

A bright, nostalgic resort with a fun, easygoing atmosphere.

Cabana Bay Beach Resort

Cabana Bay has such a distinctive personality. The moment you walk in, it feels upbeat and full of life. It is colourful without being chaotic, nostalgic without feeling like a gimmick. It’s one of those hotels where you say “okay, this is going to be a great trip”.

What I really like about Cabana Bay is how it suits almost everyone. Families love the space and the pools, couples enjoy the relaxed retro vibe, and groups of friends appreciate having so much to do without needing to leave the resort, well other than to visit the parks. It’s lively, but not overwhelming. It's just a really easy place to be.

The pool areas are a huge part of the experience. You’ve got two massive pools, a lazy river, a slide, and plenty of space to stretch out. A sunny afternoon, feels like a proper resort, music playing, people floating past on inflatables, cocktails at the pool bar… it’s just a fabulous atmosphere.

The rooms are colourful and cheerful, with that retro twist Cabana Bay is known for. If you want more space, the family suites are fantastic. They’ve got little kitchenettes, a living area, and a clever bathroom layout that makes mornings so much easier. They’re ideal for longer stays or anyone who likes having a bit of extra room.

Dining is another solid point. The food court is huge and has loads of choice, there’s a Starbucks, a couple of bars, and even a full bowling alley with its own restaurant. It’s one of the few Universal hotels where you can genuinely stay on‑site all evening and still have plenty to do.

And the location is brilliant if you’re planning to visit Volcano Bay. There’s a dedicated walking path straight to the water park, which is really handy. You can even book a room with a view of the volcano, which looks amazing lit up at night.

Transport to the other parks is simple. Regular shuttle buses run all day, and they’re quick and reliable. You’re not walking distance to Universal Studios or Islands of Adventure, but the buses make it easy.

Cabana Bay is perfect if you want a hotel that feels fun, lively and full of personality. It’s great value for what you get, and it suits such a wide range of people, not just families. If you want your hotel to feel like part of the experience rather than just somewhere to sleep, Cabana Bay is a brilliant choice.

Pros & Considerations

Pros

  • Big, lively resort with loads to do
  • Two huge pools plus a lazy river
  • Family suites with kitchenettes
  • Retro theming that feels fun and unique
  • Bowling alley and great dining options
  • Walking path to Volcano Bay
  • Early park entry included
  • Great value for the amount of facilities

Considerations

  • Not walking distance to the main parks
  • Can feel busy during peak times
  • Rooms are fun but not as modern as Aventura or the new Value hotels
  • Shuttle buses only (no water taxi)
  • Lively atmosphere may not suit those wanting something quieter
