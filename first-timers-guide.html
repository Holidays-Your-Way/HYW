First‑Timers Guide to Walt Disney World

Everything you need to know for a magical first visit.

Welcome to Your First Disney Adventure

Planning your first trip to Walt Disney World can feel overwhelming — four theme parks, two water parks, hundreds of restaurants and more magic than you can imagine. But with the right guidance, it becomes exciting, simple and stress‑free.

This guide covers everything you need to know to plan the perfect first visit, from choosing your dates to navigating the parks like a pro.

1. Choose the Best Time to Visit

The time of year you visit can make a big difference. For first‑timers, I recommend:

  • Mid‑January to early March
  • Late April to mid‑May
  • Mid‑September to mid‑November

These months offer lower crowds, comfortable weather and great value.

Read the full “When to Visit” guide

2. Choose Your Disney Resort

Staying at a Disney Resort makes your first visit easier and more magical. You’ll enjoy early park entry, complimentary transport and incredible theming.

  • Value Resorts: Great for families on a budget
  • Moderate Resorts: A balance of comfort and value
  • Deluxe Resorts: Best locations, best amenities, most magical
Explore Disney Resorts

3. Decide How Many Days You Need

For a first visit, I recommend:

  • 4 park days (one for each theme park)
  • 1–2 rest days or Disney Springs days
  • Optional: Water parks or mini golf

A 7–10 night holiday gives you the perfect balance of fun and relaxation.

4. Understand Lightning Lanes

Lightning Lanes help you skip the queues for Disney’s most popular attractions. There are three types:

  • Lightning Lane Multi Pass – book multiple rides in advance
  • Lightning Lane Single Pass – pay per ride for top attractions
  • Lightning Lane Premier Pass – unlimited access (no planning needed)
Learn more about Lightning Lanes

5. Plan Your Dining

Disney dining is a big part of the experience — and it books up quickly. First‑timers should consider:

  • 1–2 character meals
  • 1–2 Table‑Service meals
  • Quick‑Service for flexibility
Explore dining guides

6. Learn the Basics of Getting Around

Disney transport is free, easy and fun. You can travel by:

  • Disney buses
  • Monorail
  • Boats
  • Disney Skyliner

Each resort connects differently — I’ll help you choose the best one for your plans.

7. Pack Smart

Florida weather can be unpredictable. First‑timers should pack:

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Ponchos or light rain jackets
  • Portable chargers
  • Refillable water bottles
  • Sun protection
