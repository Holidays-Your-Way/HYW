Islands of Adventure – Complete Guide

The best park for coasters… with so much more to it.

Islands of Adventure opened in May 1999, and I’ve been visiting since the very beginning. 27 years this May. It’s the park people often think is “just for thrill seekers,” but that couldn’t be further from the truth. Yes, it’s home to some of the biggest coasters at Universal Orlando, and if you love thrills, this is your park.

But it also surprises people with how much there is to enjoy beyond the big rides. It’s a beautifully themed park with immersive lands, brilliant storytelling and loads to do whether you love coasters, prefer something gentler, you’re visiting with kids, with friends, or it’s just us grown ups this time.

What Islands of Adventure Is Like

Islands of Adventure is split into different “islands,” each with its own theme, atmosphere and style. It’s the kind of park where you turn a corner and suddenly feel like you’ve stepped into a completely different world.

Expect:

  • big coasters and big thrills
  • beautifully themed lands
  • brilliant water rides
  • iconic attractions like Spider‑Man and Jurassic Park
  • some of the best food at Universal
  • lots of shade and indoor spaces
  • a layout that loops around a central lagoon

It’s a park that suits:

  • thrill seekers
  • non thrill seekers
  • Potter fans
  • Marvel fans
  • dinosaur lovers
  • families
  • grown ups
  • solo travellers
  • anyone who loves immersive theming

There really is something for everyone.

The Big Attractions

Here are the rides most people prioritise — with my honest thoughts included.

Hagrid’s Magical Creatures Motorbike Adventure

One of the most popular rides in Orlando. Fast, fun, smooth and full of surprises. Queues can be long, but it’s worth every minute.

VelociCoaster

If you love coasters, this is the one. Fast, intense, smooth and beautifully designed. If you don’t like big coasters, this is a definite skip, there is no shame in that.

The Incredible Hulk Coaster

The OG. My absolute favourite ride in Universal, It's powerful, iconic and still one of the best coasters in Universal. If you love big, bold, in your face coasters, this is unmissable.

The Amazing Adventures of Spider‑Man

A brilliant, immersive dark ride. Still one of the best attractions Universal has ever created.

Jurassic Park River Adventure

A classic water ride with a big drop at the end. Great fun on a hot day, you may get very wet.

Popeye & Bluto’s Bilge‑Rat Barges

One of the best rapids rides in Orlando. You don’t just get wet, you get absolutely drenched. Brilliant fun and perfect on a hot day.

Dudley Do‑Right’s Ripsaw Falls

A wild, cartoon style log flume with a huge drop. Another one where I guarantee you’ll get SOAKED head to toe.

Flight of the Hippogriff

A gentle family coaster, great for younger riders or anyone easing into coasters.

The Seuss Landing Rides

Perfect for little ones or anyone who prefers something calm and colourful.

The Wizarding World – Hogsmeade

Hogsmeade is one of the most atmospheric areas in any theme park. Snow covered rooftops, cobbled streets, Butterbeer, the Hogwarts Express, it’s magical whether you’re a Potter fan or not.

Highlights include:

  • Hagrid’s Magical Creatures Motorbike Adventure
  • Flight of the Hippogriff
  • Hogwarts Castle
  • The shops and details
  • Butterbeer (hot, cold or frozen)

If you want quieter photos, visit early or later in the evening.

Dining at Islands of Adventure

There are some great food options here, a mix of quick service and sit down.

Three Broomsticks

Traditional British style dishes in a beautifully themed setting. Great for breakfast too.

Thunder Falls Terrace

One of the best quick service spots in the park. Great rotisserie chicken, ribs and salads.

The Burger Digs

Simple, reliable and good for picky eaters.

Croissant Moon Bakery

Lovely pastries, sandwiches and coffee. Perfect for a lighter breakfast.

Mythos Restaurant

Often called “the best theme park restaurant in the world.” It’s good, but don’t expect fine dining. A nice option It's nice if you want a sit down meal in a beautiful setting.

Note: Mythos is set to close for refurbishment in early 2026. No date has been confirmed yet, so check if it’s open during your visit.

Tips That Make Your Day Easier

These are the things I’ve learned after 27 years of visiting:

  • Start early — especially for Hagrid’s.
  • Do Hagrid’s or VelociCoaster first.
  • Water rides are best in the afternoon when it’s hottest.
  • Take breaks in the shade.
  • Don’t rush, the theming is stunning and easy to miss.
  • If you’re not a thrill seeker, there’s still loads to enjoy.

Do You Need Express Pass Here?

Express Pass is more useful here than at Universal Studios — especially if:

  • you want to avoid long queues
  • you’re visiting in peak school holidays
  • you’re travelling with younger children
  • you want a relaxed day
  • you’re staying at a hotel that includes it

Important: Express does not include Hagrid’s or VelociCoaster, so even with Express, start early.

My Honest Advice

After 27 years of visiting Islands of Adventure, here’s what I’ve learned: you don’t need to love coasters to enjoy this park. Hagrid’s is worth doing early or late. VelociCoaster is incredible, but only if you like big coasters. Hulk is iconic, and still one of the best coasters in Universal. Spider‑Man is a must do for almost everyone. Don’t underestimate the water rides, they’re brilliant. The theming is some of the best, and evenings here are gorgeous around the lagoon.

It’s a park that feels like a proper adventure, and it’s one of my favourites when you take it at your own pace.
