Universal Orlando – Your Complete Guide

Your friendly, expert introduction to everything Universal has to offer.

Universal Orlando is one of my favourite places in the world. It's a destination filled with energy, imagination and incredible experiences. I’ve been visiting Orlando for over 40 years, and I’ve been exploring Universal since it first opened its gates in June 1990. I’ve watched it grow from a single park into a full resort with four theme parks, brilliant hotels, fantastic dining and so much to explore.

If you’re planning your first Universal holiday (or even your tenth), this guide is your perfect starting point. It gives you a clear, simple overview of the entire resort, with links to deeper guides where you can explore each area in more detail.

What Is Universal Orlando?

Universal Orlando Resort is a full holiday destination made up of four theme parks, a dining and entertainment district, themed hotels and easy transport connecting everything. It’s designed so you can stay, play, eat, relax and explore without ever needing to leave the resort.

Universal is known for:

  • immersive lands
  • brilliant storytelling
  • big thrills
  • incredible theming
  • great food
  • a fun, energetic atmosphere

The Four Theme Parks

Here’s a quick overview of each park, with links to the full guides.

Universal Studios Florida

The original Universal park. It is full of immersive lands, indoor attractions, brilliant shows and that classic Universal energy. A great “first day” park.

Explore Universal Studios

Islands of Adventure

Adventure, thrills and amazing theming. From Jurassic Park to Marvel to Hogsmeade, this park is packed with iconic rides and stunning views.

Explore Islands of Adventure

Epic Universe

Universal’s newest, most breathtaking park. Immersive lands, incredible food and next generation rides. It becomes especially magical at night.

Explore Epic Universe

Volcano Bay

A tropical water theme park with beaches, rivers, slides and cabanas. The perfect “reset day” during your holiday, and one of my personal favourites.

Explore Volcano Bay

CityWalk – Dining, Drinks & Entertainment

CityWalk is Universal’s dining and entertainment district. It's the place you’ll walk through every day on your way to the parks. It’s brilliant in the evenings when the lights come on and the atmosphere picks up.

Explore Dining

Universal Hotels – Where to Stay

Universal has a range of hotels to suit every style and budget.

  • Premier Hotels: Best perks, best locations, Express Pass included.
  • Preferred Hotels: Great theming and comfort.
  • Prime Value Hotels: Modern, fun and budget‑friendly.
  • Value Hotels: Affordable and great for families.
Explore Universal Hotels

Express Pass – A Simple Overview

Express Pass lets you skip the regular queue on most rides. Premier hotels include it, and you can buy it separately. It’s not always needed with a 14 day ticket, but it’s a game changer in peak season.
