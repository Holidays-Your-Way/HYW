Universal Orlando for First Timers

A friendly, honest guide to planning your first Universal holiday with confidence.

Planning your first trip to Universal Orlando can feel exciting, overwhelming, and a little bit chaotic, and honestly, that’s completely normal. With four theme parks, endless dining options, brilliant transport, and so much to see, it’s hard to know where to begin.

But you’re in safe hands. I’ve been visiting Orlando for over 40 years, and Universal since it first opened in 1990. I’ve watched the parks grow, change, and evolve into the incredible destination it is today. This guide is designed to give you the kind of real world advice only decades of experience can offer.

And because UK guests get the 14 day Universal Explorer Ticket, you already have the biggest advantage of all: time. No rushing. No cramming. No pressure. Just exploring at your own pace.

Universal Orlando’s Four Theme Parks

Universal Orlando has four theme parks — three traditional theme parks and one water theme park. Each has its own personality, atmosphere and must do experiences.

Universal Studios Florida

Universal Studios is the original park, it's a place where film sets, immersive lands and big energy attractions blend together. It’s packed with indoor rides (great for escaping the heat), brilliant shows, street entertainment and some of the most iconic Universal experiences.

Why first timers love it:

  • Easy to navigate and a great “first day” park.
  • Lots of indoor attractions and air conditioning.
  • Great balance of thrills and storytelling.
  • Diagon Alley is brilliant for Harry Potter fans.
  • Classic Universal energy and atmosphere.

Islands of Adventure

Islands of Adventure is a beautifully designed park with huge landscapes, iconic rides and some of the best views in Orlando. Every land feels like stepping into a different world. From Jurassic Park to Marvel Super Hero Island to the snowy rooftops of Hogsmeade.

Why first timers love it:

  • Home to some of the best coasters.
  • Stunning theming and photo spots everywhere.
  • Big, bold, immersive lands to explore.
  • A real sense of adventure and discovery.
  • Water rides where you don’t just get wet — you get soaked!

Epic Universe

Epic Universe is a next generation theme park. It is visually stunning, incredibly immersive and full of rides and lands you won’t find anywhere else. It’s designed to feel like stepping into different worlds, each with its own atmosphere, food, music and storytelling.

Why first timers love it:

  • Brand new, fresh and exciting.
  • Incredibly immersive themed lands.
  • Some of Universal’s best food and snacks.
  • Magical nighttime atmosphere with beautiful lighting.
  • Celestial Park is one of the most beautiful areas in any theme park.

Volcano Bay (Water Theme Park)

Volcano Bay isn’t “just a waterpark”, Universal calls it a water theme park, and it genuinely feels like a tropical resort. With a towering volcano, rivers, beaches, cabanas and warm, laid back theming, it’s the perfect reset day during your holiday.

Why first timers love it:

  • The lazy river is unbelievably relaxing.
  • The fearless river is brilliant fun if you like something wilder.
  • Stunning views of the volcano from the beach.
  • TapuTapu makes queueing and access easy.
  • A great break from the pace of the main parks.

And yes, the lazy river is a favourite for many adults (myself included). Pop your phone in a waterproof case and float around while “working hard”, or making photo and video memories you and your family will treasure forever.

Best Time of Year to Visit

There’s no single “perfect” time, but here’s a simple overview:

  • January–March: Cooler weather, lower humidity, manageable crowds.
  • April–May: Warm, pleasant, great for pool days.
  • September–October: Hot but often quieter, plus Halloween Horror Nights.
  • November–mid December: Christmas decorations, cooler weather, lovely atmosphere. I spent Christmas Day 2025 in Volcano Bay, it was perfect.

UK school holidays (Easter, summer, October half‑term and Christmas) are busier, but with a 14 day ticket you can spread your park days out and avoid the busiest times of day.

How to Get Around Universal Orlando

Universal is easy to get around, especially if you’re staying at a Universal hotel.

  • Walking paths: Some Universal hotels are within walking distance of the parks.
  • Water taxis: A scenic, relaxing way to travel between select hotels and CityWalk.
  • Shuttle buses: Run frequently and get you to the parks quickly.
  • Parking: Standard parking at Universal Orlando is currently $32 per day. All parking prices are subject to change.

Express Pass Explained (Simply)

Express Pass lets you skip the regular queue on most rides.

  • Premier hotels include it: Stay at Hard Rock Hotel, Portofino Bay or Royal Pacific and you’ll get Express Pass for every day of your stay, including check‑in and check‑out days.
  • You can buy it separately: Prices vary by date and can be expensive on busy days.
  • Do you need it? With a 14 day ticket, not always, but in peak season it might be a game changer for getting more done with less waiting.

Where to Stay

Universal has several hotel categories, each with its own perks and price point.

  • Premier hotels: Best perks, best locations, Express Pass included.
  • Preferred hotels: Great quality and theming, no Express Pass.
  • Prime Value hotels: Modern, fun and budget friendly.
  • Value hotels: Affordable, clean and great for families.

Best Dining for First Timers

Here are some easy, reliable and crowd‑pleasing options to start with.

CityWalk

  • Bigfire: Modern American, open fire cooking, great cocktails.
  • The Cowfish: Burgers, sushi and a fun atmosphere.
  • Antojitos: Mexican food with live music.
  • Toothsome Chocolate Emporium: Themed dining with big desserts.
  • VIVO Italian Kitchen: Fresh pasta and a good wine list.

In the Parks

  • Leaky Cauldron (Universal Studios) – Comfort food in a brilliant setting.
  • Three Broomsticks (Islands of Adventure) – Hearty meals and great theming.
  • Finnegan’s Bar & Grill (Universal Studios) – Hearty food, great atmosphere, a favourite with returning guests.
  • Toadstool Café (Epic Universe) – Fun theming and surprisingly good food.
  • Atlantic Restaurant (Epic Universe) – Calmer, more elegant dining.

Hotel Favourites

  • Strong Water Tavern (Sapphire Falls) – Fantastic rum bar and small plates.
  • Amatista Cookhouse (Sapphire Falls) – Relaxed, varied menu.
  • Jake’s American Bar (Royal Pacific) – Classic, easy American dining.

Tips for Avoiding Overwhelm

  • Start early: Mornings are cooler and quieter.
  • Take breaks: Especially in the midday heat.
  • Don’t zig‑zag: Do one land at a time instead of jumping all over the park.
  • Use the app: Check wait times, maps and mobile ordering.
  • Stay hydrated: Florida heat is no joke.
  • Don’t try to do everything: You have 14 days, so spread it out.
  • Enjoy the evenings: Cooler, calmer and beautifully lit.

My Honest Advice

Your first Universal holiday should feel exciting, not stressful. With a 14 day UK ticket, you have the luxury of time, so take it slow, enjoy the atmosphere and let the parks unfold naturally.

Universal is full of magic, energy and surprises, and your first visit will be one you remember forever. And if you’re not sure where to start, or you’d like personalised advice, I’m always happy to share what I’ve learned from over 40 years of visiting Orlando and Universal since 1990.
