Aventura Hotel

A sleek, modern high‑rise with a calm, grown‑up atmosphere.

Aventura Hotel

Aventura has a completely different feel from the other Universal hotels. It’s modern, minimalist and surprisingly calm. If you like clean lines, big windows and a hotel that feels more “city chic” than “theme park resort”, Aventura is a brilliant fit.

The lobby sets the tone straight away: bright, airy and uncluttered. It’s a lovely contrast to the energy of the parks, and that’s exactly why so many adults, couples and older families gravitate towards it. Children are absolutely welcome, but the overall vibe is definitely more relaxed and grownup.

The rooms follow the same style: modern, simple and cleverly designed. Floor to ceiling windows make them feel bigger than they are, and the views can be fantastic, especially if you’re lucky enough to grab one facing Volcano Bay or the skyline. Everything feels fresh and streamlined, which is great if you prefer a tidy, minimalist space.

One of Aventura’s biggest highlights is the rooftop bar, Bar 17 Bistro. It’s genuinely one of the nicest spots on Universal property. It offers amazing views, great cocktails and a really relaxed atmosphere. It’s perfect for a quiet drink at the end of the day, and it’s one of the reasons Aventura appeals so much to adults and couples.

The pool area is simple but lovely. It’s not themed like Cabana Bay or Sapphire Falls, but it’s clean, calm and has plenty of space to unwind. There’s also a splash area for younger children, so families are still well catered for.

Dining is easy and flexible. The food hall has a good mix of options, and as with the others, there’s a Starbucks in the lobby for early mornings. If you want something a bit more special, you can always pop up to the rooftop or wander over to Sapphire Falls next door, the two hotels are connected by a walking path.

Location‑wise, Aventura is in a brilliant spot. You can walk to Volcano Bay in just a few minutes, and the shuttle buses to the other parks are quick and reliable. If you like being close to everything without being in the middle of the noise, it’s a great balance.

Aventura is perfect if you want a hotel that feels modern, calm and a little more grownup. It’s stylish without being expensive, relaxed without being boring, and a great choice for adults, couples and families who prefer a quieter base with easy access to everything.

Pros & Considerations

Pros

  • Modern, sleek, minimalist design
  • Rooftop bar with amazing views
  • Calm, relaxed atmosphere
  • Walking distance to Volcano Bay
  • Great for adults, couples and older families
  • Quick shuttle buses to the parks
  • Early park entry included
  • Food hall and Starbucks in the lobby

Considerations

  • Rooms are on the smaller side
  • Pool area is simple compared to themed resorts
  • Not walking distance to Universal Studios or Islands of Adventure
  • Less suited to families wanting lots of on‑site activities
  • No water taxi
