Disney FAQs

Your most common Disney questions, answered simply.

Planning a Disney Holiday?

Whether it’s your first visit or your tenth, Disney planning can feel overwhelming — especially with little ones, teens or a big family group. I’ve put together the most common questions I get from UK guests to help make things clearer and easier.

And of course, if you ever want personalised help, I’m always here to guide you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need Lightning Lanes?

Lightning Lanes are optional, but they can save you time on the most popular rides. I’ll always advise you honestly based on your family, your dates and your park plans. Some rides are worth it, others aren’t — and many can be done easily by arriving early.

Are water parks included in UK Disney tickets?

Yes! UK tickets include access to Disney’s water parks. Disney usually has one water park open at a time for maintenance, so check with me and I’ll confirm whether Typhoon Lagoon or Blizzard Beach will be open during your stay.

How many days do I need at Disney?

Most UK guests visit for 7–14 days. This gives you time to enjoy all four parks, a water park, Disney Springs and some relaxing resort days. You can explore my Disney Itineraries for ideas.

What’s the best age for Disney?

Disney is magical at every age — toddlers, teens and adults all enjoy it differently. I’ve created guides for each: Toddlers, Teens and Adults.

Do I need to stay in a Disney Resort?

You don’t have to, but staying on‑site gives you extra perks, easier transport and more Disney magic. There are Value, Moderate and Deluxe options to suit every budget — explore them on the Disney Resorts page.

How far in advance should I book?

Disney holidays book up quickly, especially for school holidays. I always recommend booking as early as possible to secure the best prices, resort availability and flight options.

Do I need dining reservations?

Some restaurants book up fast — especially character dining and popular spots like California Grill or Ohana. I’ll help you choose the best options for your family and advise when to book.

Is Disney good for adults?

Absolutely. EPCOT alone is incredible for adults, and Disney has amazing dining, cocktails, lounges and luxury resorts. You can read more on my Disney For Adults page.

Can you help me plan my days?

Yes — I can create a personalised plan based on your family, your pace and what you want to experience. My Disney Itineraries are a great starting point.
