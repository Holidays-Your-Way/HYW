Loews Royal Pacific Resort

A beautifully themed, island inspired resort with Unlimited Express Pass included.

Loews Royal Pacific Resort

Royal Pacific is one of those hotels that immediately feels special. It has this gorgeous South Pacific theme running through it. With lush landscaping, wooden walkways, soft lighting, and that gentle “tropical retreat” feeling that makes you breathe out a little the moment you arrive. It’s warm, welcoming and just a lovely place to stay.

What really sets Royal Pacific apart is how effortlessly it balances everything. It’s relaxed without being sleepy, polished without being formal, and family friendly without feeling chaotic. Couples love it, families love it, adults love it. It’s one of those rare resorts that genuinely suits everyone.

And here’s something many people don’t realise: Royal Pacific has Jurassic World Kids’ Suites. These are brilliant for families who want extra space and something fun for the children. The suites include a spacious main bedroom for adults and a separate kids’ room themed around Jurassic World, complete with gyrosphere type bunk beds and playful details. The kids’ room only connects to the adult room (not the hallway), which us parents really appreciate. They’re fun, practical and ideal for longer stays or families who simply want more breathing room.

And of course, the big headline: Staying here includes Unlimited Express Pass for your entire party. That alone can transform a Universal holiday, especially at busy times of year. It’s one of the main reasons Royal Pacific is such incredible value compared to buying Express separately.

The pool area is lovely, it is tropical, spacious and beautifully designed, with a sandy beach area and plenty of room to unwind. It’s lively enough to feel fun, but never overwhelming. There’s also a great pool bar and a play area for younger children.

The rooms are fresh, modern and comfortable, with subtle island inspired touches. They’re not flashy, but they’re well designed and feel calm and inviting, which is exactly what you want after a long day in the parks.

Dining here offers another strong point. Islands Dining Room is relaxed and family friendly, Jake’s American Bar is great for a casual evening, and the Wantilan Luau (on select nights) is a brilliant experience if you want something a bit different. There’s also a quick service marketplace and a Starbucks for early mornings.

One of the best things about Royal Pacific is the water taxi. It’s such a peaceful, scenic way to get to CityWalk and the parks, and it’s surprisingly quick. You can also walk to the parks if you prefer, the walk is lovely and takes around 10–15 minutes.

Royal Pacific is perfect if you want a resort that feels relaxing, tropical and a little more refined. But with the massive perk of Unlimited Express Pass included, and the option of themed family suites that give you both space and fun.

Pros & Considerations

Pros

  • Unlimited Express Pass included
  • Jurassic World Kids’ Suites available
  • Gorgeous tropical theming and landscaping
  • Water taxi and walking path to the parks
  • Lovely pool with sandy beach area
  • Great dining options, including the Wantilan Luau
  • Calm, elegant atmosphere that suits adults and families
  • Excellent value compared to buying Express separately
  • Early park entry included

Considerations

  • More expensive than Prime Value and Moderate resorts
  • Rooms are comfortable but not as luxurious as Hard Rock or Portofino
  • Can feel busy during peak seasons
  • Some rooms are a longer walk from the lobby or water taxi
  • No Volcano Bay walking path
