Universal Orlando Resorts

A simple, friendly guide to every Universal hotel.

Universal has a brilliant range of hotels, and the good news is that there’s something for every family, every budget and every style of holiday. Whether you want great value, something modern and stylish, or a hotel that includes Express Pass, this guide will help you understand the differences in a simple, friendly way.

I’ve stayed in Universal hotels a few times over the years, and each one has its own personality. This page gives you a clear overview of what to expect, with a few personal insights to help you choose the right one for your family.

How Universal Hotels Are Grouped

Universal splits its hotels into four categories:

  • Value: the most affordable
  • Prime Value: great value with extra features
  • Preferred: mid‑range with lovely theming
  • Premier: top tier, includes Express Pass

Epic Universe also has its own hotels, but they still fit into these categories — so I’ve included them where they naturally belong. I’ve also added a little note beside any hotel that’s closest to Epic Universe, as this is something many families want to know.

Value Hotels

Bright, relaxed and brilliant for families on a budget.

Value hotels image placeholder

These hotels offer the best prices while still giving you all the benefits of staying on‑site, including early park entry and easy transport. Two Value hotels also offer large two‑bedroom family suites, which is one of the reasons they’re so popular with bigger families.

Surfside Inn & Suites

A bright, beach‑themed hotel with a relaxed feel. Great for families, especially with the two‑bedroom suites. It’s simple, friendly and excellent value.

Dockside Inn & Suites

Similar to Surfside but slightly larger, with big family suites and a laid‑back atmosphere. It’s one of the best value options in Orlando for families who want space without the price tag.

Stella Nova Resort

A stylish new Value hotel with a cosmic theme. Closest to Epic Universe. It feels modern and fresh, and it’s a great option if you want to be near the new park without needing to break the bank.

Terra Luna Resort

Another new Value hotel with a clean, modern design. Close to Epic Universe. It has a calm, contemporary feel and works well for families who want something simple but new.

Prime Value Hotels

Fun, modern and great for families.

Prime Value hotels image placeholder

These hotels offer more features and theming than the Value hotels, but still at a very good price. Cabana Bay also has family suites, which is one of the reasons it’s so popular with larger families.

Cabana Bay Beach Resort

A colourful, retro‑themed hotel with a proper holiday atmosphere. Families love it here, the pools, lazy river and bowling alley make it feel like a resort in its own right. It’s lively, fun and great value, and the family suites are ideal if you want extra space.

Aventura Hotel

Modern, stylish and a little quieter than the other hotels. It has a sleek, contemporary feel and a lovely rooftop bar with great views. A good choice if you prefer something calm and modern.

Preferred Hotels

A lovely mid‑range option with beautiful theming.

Preferred hotels image placeholder

Sapphire Falls Resort

A beautifully themed Caribbean style hotel with a relaxed, tropical feel. It has a gorgeous pool area and a slightly more premium atmosphere than the Value and Prime Value hotels. A great middle ground choice. It also has family suites, which many people don’t realise, making it ideal if you want more space without stepping up to Premier prices.

Helios Grand Hotel

A stunning new hotel with beautiful theming and a more luxurious feel than the other Preferred options. Closest to Epic Universe. It’s ideal if you want to be right next to the new park, you will literally walk out the door and into the park. But remember that Express Pass is not included here.

Premier Hotels (Express Pass Included)

The top tier, and the only hotels that include Express Pass.

Premier hotels image placeholder

If you’re visiting during busy times, staying in a Premier hotel can save you hours of queueing. The hotels themselves are also beautifully themed and feel more luxurious. All three Premier hotels also offer family suites, though they’re not always widely advertised.

Royal Pacific Resort

A calm, tropical themed hotel with lovely gardens and a peaceful atmosphere. It’s one of the easiest Premier hotels to get around, and the theming is beautiful. Royal Pacific also has Jurassic World Kids’ Suites, which are brilliant for families. Fun for the children and practical for parents. Express Pass included.

Hard Rock Hotel

Lively, fun and closest to Universal Studios. It has a great pool and a bold, energetic feel. Perfect if you like a bit of atmosphere. Hard Rock also offers spacious suites, ideal for families who want more room. Express Pass included.

Portofino Bay Hotel

Elegant, spacious and themed like an Italian seaside village. It’s propbably the most luxurious of the Universal hotels and has a very relaxed, premium feel. Portofino has some of the best family suites at Universal, including Despicable Me themed kids’ suites and large non‑themed suites. Express Pass included. Portofino Bay is my personal favourite Premier Hotel. I just love everything about this hotel.

Getting Around: Transport at Universal Hotels

All Universal hotels offer:

  • free shuttle buses
  • walking paths (from some hotels)
  • water taxis (from Sapphire Falls and Premier hotels)

Transport is quick, easy and reliable. It is one of the biggest perks of staying on‑site.

My Honest Advice

You can’t go wrong with any Universal hotel. They all have great transport and early entry.

  • Value hotels are brilliant for families who want space without spending too much.
  • Cabana Bay is perfect if you want a lively, fun resort feel.
  • Aventura is great if you prefer something modern and calm.
  • Sapphire Falls is a lovely middle ground option.
  • Helios Grand is ideal if you want to be as close as possible to Epic Universe.
  • Premier hotels are worth it for Express Pass alone during busy times.
  • Choose the hotel that matches your style, not just your budget.
