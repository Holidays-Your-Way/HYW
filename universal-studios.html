Universal Studios Florida – Complete Guide

Step into the movies with a warm, expert helping hand.

Universal Studios Florida is where film, TV and storytelling come to life, but not in a cheesy “Hollywood lights” way, but in a way that feels immersive, clever and full of energy. It’s the original Universal Orlando park, and it still has that iconic “you’re really here” feeling the moment you walk through the gates.

I’ve been visiting Orlando for over 40 years, and I’ve been exploring Universal since it first opened its gates in 1990. Universal Studios has changed a lot over the years, but it remains one of the most atmospheric, nostalgic and enjoyable parks to spend a day in Especially if you love a mix of rides, shows and classic Universal charm.

This guide gives you a clear, friendly overview of the park, with my honest advice woven throughout.

What Universal Studios Is Like

Universal Studios is designed like a real working film studio, with different “neighbourhoods” themed around movies, TV and iconic characters. It’s colourful, easy to walk, full of atmosphere and has a great mix of rides for all ages and travel styles. Whether you’re visiting with kids, with friends, or it’s just us grown ups this time.

If you love:

  • Harry Potter
  • Minions
  • Transformers
  • The Simpsons
  • Big, cinematic rides

…this is your park.

It’s also a park where planning makes a big difference, and that’s where my experience comes in.

The Big Attractions

Here are the rides and experiences most people tend to prioritise, with my honest thoughts included.

Harry Potter and the Escape from Gringotts

A brilliant, story driven ride through the Gringotts vaults. If you’re a Harry Potter fan, or even if you’re not (like me… shhhh, don’t tell anyone) then this is a must do.

Despicable Me Minion Mayhem

Perfect for younger children. Fun, silly and very popular first thing in the morning.

Transformers: The Ride 3D

Fast‑paced, immersive and great for older kids, teens and adults who love simulator rides.

The Simpsons Ride

A colourful simulator that fans of the show will love. BUT, motion sickness is real on this one. I love rides, the bigger the better, and I never get motion sickness… but this one even gets me.

Men in Black: Alien Attack

A really fun interactive shooting ride. My clients always tell me this becomes a surprise favourite.

E.T. Adventure

A nostalgic classic, it's gentle, sweet and perfect for younger children. Older kids might find it tame, but it’s a lovely break from the bigger rides.

Shows Worth Seeing

The Bourne Stuntacular

Genuinely impressive and well worth fitting into your day.

Animal Actors

A lovely option if it’s running during your visit.

The Horror Make‑Up Show

A surprisingly good one, it's funny, clever and great for older kids and adults. Not ideal for little ones or anyone squeamish. It’s currently closed, but not permanently, so fingers crossed it’s open during your trip.

Coming Soon – Fast & Furious Coaster

Hollywood Rip Ride Rockit has now been retired and demolished. In its place, Universal is building a brand new Fast & Furious coaster. Opening date is still to be confirmed, but it’s definitely one to watch if you love big coasters or that franchise.

The Wizarding World – Diagon Alley

This is one of the most beautifully themed areas in any theme park. Even if you’re not a huge Harry Potter fan, it’s well worth exploring.

Highlights include:

  • Escape from Gringotts
  • Ollivanders wand experience
  • Butterbeer
  • Knockturn Alley
  • The fire breathing dragon

If you want photos without the crowds, visit early or later in the evening.

Dining at Universal Studios

There’s a good mix of quick service and sit down options. Some of mine and my clients favourites include:

Leaky Cauldron

Traditional British style dishes in a beautifully themed setting.

Louie’s Italian Restaurant

Great for mixed groups, it is simple, reliable and good value.

Today Café

Lovely sandwiches, pastries and coffee. A great stop if you want something lighter.

Fast Food Boulevard (Simpsons)

Fun, colourful and great for picky eaters.

Finnegan’s Bar & Grill

A proper favourite. It has great food, great drinks and a relaxed pub feel.

Tips That Make Your Day Easier

  • Start early — the first hour is always the quietest.
  • Do Minions or Gringotts first — these get busy quickly.
  • Use the Hogwarts Express — it’s not just transport, it’s an attraction.
  • Take breaks in the shade — there are lots of indoor attractions.
  • Don’t rush — this park has lots of little details that are easy to miss.

Do You Need Express Pass Here?

You don’t need Express every day, and some visitors don’t need it at all.

But it can be helpful if:

  • you’re visiting in peak school holidays
  • you’re travelling with younger children
  • you want a relaxed day without rushing
  • you’re staying at a hotel that includes it

If you’re unsure, I can look at your dates and give you honest advice.

My Honest Advice

After 35 years of visiting Universal Studios, here’s what I’ve learned: you don’t need to do everything. Choose what matters to you and your group. Gringotts is worth doing early or late. The Bourne Stuntacular is one of the best theme park shows. Don’t underestimate how hot it can get, plan indoor breaks. If this was my trip, I’d always do Diagon Alley twice: once in the day and once at night. Express Pass can help, but it’s not essential for everyone. The park feels very different depending on the time of day, evenings are fantastic.

Universal Studios is a brilliant park when you take it at your own pace.
