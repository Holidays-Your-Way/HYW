Busch Gardens Tampa Bay

Busch Gardens Tampa Bay

World‑class coasters, animals & African‑themed adventures

Your friendly guide to one of Florida’s most thrilling parks

A Thrill‑Seekers Paradise With a Safari Twist

Busch Gardens Tampa Bay is one of Florida’s most underrated parks — a brilliant mix of world‑class coasters, African‑themed areas, animal habitats and lush, tropical landscaping. It’s a proper “big day out”, especially for families with teens or anyone who loves a good coaster.

It’s about an hour from Orlando, but absolutely worth the trip. Think of it as a blend of SeaWorld’s thrill rides and Animal Kingdom’s safari atmosphere — but with its own personality and some of the best coasters in the state.

The Big Coasters – Some of Florida’s Best

Busch Gardens is famous for its coasters — fast, intense and beautifully designed. If you love thrills, this is your park.

Iron Gwazi

The star of the park. A hybrid coaster with huge drops, wild speed and smooth, twisting airtime. Consistently ranked one of the best coasters in the world.

SheiKra

A 200‑foot vertical drop coaster where you hang over the edge before plunging straight down. Terrifying, brilliant and surprisingly smooth.

Montu

A classic inverted coaster with big loops and fast transitions. Intense but iconic — a must‑do for coaster fans.

Cheetah Hunt

A long, multi‑launch coaster that’s fast, smooth and perfect for families who want thrills without going too extreme.

Family Rides – Fun Without the Fear

Busch Gardens isn’t just for thrill‑seekers — there are plenty of rides that families can enjoy together.

Cobra’s Curse

A spinning family coaster with a vertical lift and fun twists. Great for kids who want something exciting but not too intense.

Serengeti Express Train

A gentle train ride through the park’s savannah areas — perfect for a break and great for little ones.

Animal Habitats – A Safari in Florida

Busch Gardens has a strong focus on conservation and animal care, with large, open habitats and plenty of opportunities to learn.

  • Serengeti Plain: giraffes, zebras, antelope and more
  • Tiger Trail: beautifully themed tiger habitat
  • Gorilla & Chimpanzee areas: peaceful and educational
  • Bird Gardens: colourful, calm and great for photos
  • Elephant habitat: a guest favourite

You can also book up‑close tours, including feeding giraffes — a brilliant add‑on for families.

Shows & Entertainment

Busch Gardens offers a mix of live entertainment, seasonal events and animal presentations.

  • Animal Care Center: see real veterinary procedures and learn about conservation
  • Live music: dotted around the park, especially during festivals
  • Seasonal shows: Christmas Town, Mardi Gras, Food & Wine Festival

Food & Drink – Plenty of Choice

Busch Gardens has a good mix of quick‑service restaurants, snacks and festival booths.

  • Zagora Café: burgers, wraps and salads
  • Dragon Fire Grill: big portions and lots of variety
  • Twisted Tails Pretzels: great for snacks
  • Ice cream & drinks: found throughout the park

During festivals, the food booths are genuinely excellent — especially the spring and Christmas events.

Planning Tips – The Good Stuff I Tell My Clients

  • Arrive early: Iron Gwazi and SheiKra get busy fast.
  • Use the train: it’s a great way to rest and still see animals.
  • It’s a big park: wear comfy shoes and take breaks.
  • Check the weather: Tampa storms can pause coasters.
  • Plan your route: the park layout is wide, not circular.
  • Great for teens: loads of thrill rides.

Who Busch Gardens Is Best For

  • Thrill‑seekers and coaster fans
  • Families with older kids or teens
  • Animal lovers who enjoy safari‑style experiences
  • Guests wanting something different from Orlando’s main parks
  • Anyone with a 14‑day ticket looking for extra value

Busch Gardens is a brilliant mix of thrills, animals and atmosphere — a proper adventure day that feels totally different from the Orlando parks.
