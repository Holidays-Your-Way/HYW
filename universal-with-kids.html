Universal Orlando With Younger Children

A friendly, honest guide to making Universal magical for little ones.

Universal Orlando is brilliant for younger children, a lot more than people realise. With colourful lands, gentle rides, brilliant shows, splash areas, character meet and greets and plenty of places to take a break, it’s a fantastic destination for families of all ages.

This guide walks you through the best rides, shows, dining, pacing and tips to help your little ones have a relaxed, happy day in the parks.

Best Rides for Younger Children

Universal Studios Florida

DreamWorks Land is the main dedicated area for younger kids. It's colourful, playful and full of gentle attractions.

DreamWorks Land

Trolls Village
  • Trollercoaster – A gentle family coaster. Height: 36" (92cm)
  • Trolls Play Area – Slides, nets, climbing and interactive fun. No height requirement
  • Meet Poppy, Branch & Guy Diamond
Shrek’s Swamp
  • Shrek’s Swamp for Little Ogres – Toddler friendly play zone. No height requirement
  • Water play areas – Great for cooling off.
  • Meet Shrek, Fiona & Donkey
Kung Fu Panda: Training Camp
  • Interactive play zone – Water cannons, obstacle course elements. No height requirement
  • Meet Po

Other Great Rides

  • Despicable Me Minion Mayhem – Silly, colourful fun. Height: 40" (102cm)
  • Kang & Kodos’ Twirl ’n’ Hurl – Gentle spinning. 48" (122cm) to ride alone
  • Hogwarts Express – A calm, magical journey. No height requirement
  • ET Adventure – A classic dark ride. Height: 34" (86cm)

Islands of Adventure

This park has some of the best themed areas for younger children.

  • The Cat in the Hat – Storybook dark ride. Height: 36" (92cm)
  • One Fish, Two Fish, Red Fish, Blue Fish – Gentle spinning. No height requirement
  • Caro‑Seuss‑el – Whimsical carousel. No height requirement
  • Flight of the Hippogriff – Family coaster. Height: 39" (99cm)
  • Pteranodon Flyers – Gentle flying ride. Child must be 36–56"

Epic Universe

Epic Universe is full of brilliant family friendly areas.

Celestial Park

  • Stargazer Carousel – Gentle carousel. No height requirement
  • Constellation Carousel – Another calm option. No height requirement

Isle of Berk

  • Dragon Racer’s Rally – Fun but not intense. Height: 40" (102cm)
  • Fyre Drill – Water‑blaster ride. No height requirement
  • Hiccup’s Wing Gliders – Gentle flying ride. Height: 36" (92cm)

Super Nintendo World

  • Yoshi’s Adventure – Slow, colourful ride. No height requirement

Volcano Bay

Perfect for younger children who love water.

  • Tot Tiki Reef – Splash area. No height requirement
  • Runamukka Reef – Slides and water play. No height requirement
  • Kopiko Wai Winding River – Lazy river. No height requirement
  • Wave Village – Gentle waves. No height requirement

Best Shows for Younger Children

  • Animal Actors (if running)
  • The Bourne Stuntacular (visually amazing but loud)
  • Frog Choir (Hogsmeade)
  • Triwizard Spirit Rally (Hogsmeade)
  • DreamWorks character shows & dance parties
  • Meet and greets across all parks

Epic Universe

Volcano Bay

  • Bambu – Quick and easy.
  • Kohola Reef – Bigger meals.

Best Strategy for a Smooth Day

  • Start early, finish early – Little ones cope best with mornings.
  • Take regular breaks – Every 60–90 minutes is ideal.
  • Use shaded areas in the afternoon.
  • Stick to one land at a time – Avoid zig zagging.
  • Bring snacks – Queues are easier with something to nibble.
  • Use Rider Swap – One adult rides while the other waits calmly with the child.
  • Don’t force the big rides – You have 14 days, there’s no rush.

What to Avoid With Younger Children

  • Long outdoor queues in the afternoon.
  • Intense rides like Hulk, VelociCoaster.
  • Loud or dark attractions if your child is sensitive.
  • Over planning — flexibility is your best friend.
  • Skipping breaks — meltdowns happen when kids are overtired.

My Honest Advice

Universal is genuinely wonderful for younger children. It's colourful, imaginative, and full of gentle attractions they’ll love. The key is to take your time, follow the natural flow of each park, and build in plenty of breaks.

With 14 days to explore, you don’t need to rush anything. Let them set the pace, enjoy the little moments, and soak up the magic.
